What is a robotic gripper capable of manipulating a hexagonal-base pyramid with variable parameters?

The goal of this project is to design a gripper able to grasp one kind of geometrical object, which will have variable dimensions. Basing on those parameters, the gripper must adapt to the geometry in order to perform an optimal grab. The given object is a hexagonal-based regular pyramid, whose key parameters are the side length and the height of the pyramid. The first one can go from 40 to 60 mm, and the second from 50 to 80 mm. Additionally, the pyramid is made of steel and is hollow inside, forming a 1 mm-thick shell.




CALCULATIONS:

In the following lines we proceed to calculate some basic magnitude of the geometrical object, such as its volume, weight or its gravity centre’s threshold.

The volume and weight will be maximum for maximum side size and hight. The area of the base follows the expression:


\( A_{Base} = \frac{6 \cdot d \cdot \left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot d \right)}{2} \)


And in the same way, we can get the formula for the pyramid’s volume:


\( V_{pyramid} = \frac{A_{base} \cdot h}{3} \)


The already sectioned sketches of the object, show the disposition of the figure comparing the two shells, we can extract the same relations between them:



\( d = \) external shell side size,   \( d1 = \) internal shell side size

\( h = \) external shell height,   \( h1 = \) internal shell height

\( d1 = d - 1.155, \quad h1 = h - 2.836 \)

\( r_i = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot d \)   \( r_u = d \)

\( Pyramid_{volume} = d \cdot r_i \cdot h \)


Combining all the expressions we can obtain the value of the volume in mm³:


\[ EffectiveVolume = \frac{(6 \cdot d \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot h) - (6 \cdot (d - 1.155) \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}(d - 1.155)}{2} \cdot (h - 2.836))}{3} \]


\( EffectiveVolume = 36029.414 mm^3 \)


\[ m_{max} = 36029.414 [mm^3] \cdot 7.85 \cdot 10^{-6} \frac{kg}{mm^3} = 0.2828 Kg \]


Calculation of gravity centre. If we divide the pyramid in two axes: vertical (y) and horizontal (x), we can calculate \( \alpha \), \( x_{gc} \) and \( y_{gc} \):

\( x_{gc} = \frac{3}{4} \cdot h \cdot \tan(\alpha) \)

\( \alpha = arctan\left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{d}{h} \right) \)

\( y_{gc} = \frac{3}{4} \cdot h \)


We can calculate the threshold between the two pairs of values:

\( h_{max} = 80, \quad d_{max} = 60 \)

Then:

\( \alpha_1 = arctan\left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{60}{80} \right) = 36.67° \)

\( x_{gc_{max}} = \frac{3}{4} \cdot 80 \cdot \tan(36.67) = 38.97 mm \)

\( y_{gc_{max}} = \frac{3}{4} \cdot 80 = 60 mm \)

We do the same with the other values:

\( h_{min} = 50, \quad d_{min} = 40 \)

\( \alpha_2 = arctan\left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{40}{50} \right) = 38.57^\circ \)

\( x_{gc_{min}} = \frac{3}{4} \cdot 50 \cdot \tan(38.57) = 25.98mm \)

\( y_{gc_{min}} = \frac{3}{4} \cdot 50 = 37.5mm \)

The angular threshold for link number 6 is -43.89, -66.59, measuring from the vertical negative axis starting from that link.



FORCES:

In order to correctly grasp the object, it is necessary to calculate the suction force of each suction cup. We are going to pre-set the maximum acceleration in 5m/s^2, it is possible to obtain that force by the following expressions:

\( F_g = m \cdot (g \cdot a) \cdot s \cdot \left( cos\alpha + \frac{sin\alpha}{\mu} \right) = 0.2828 \cdot (9.8 + 5) \cdot 2 \cdot (cos(43.89) + \frac{sin(43.89)}{0.5}) \)

\( F_g = 17.64N \)

\( k = \left( 1 + \frac{r}{R} \right) = 1 + \frac{11.25}{11} = 2.02 \)

\( F_{r1} = F_g \cdot k \)

\( F_{r1} = 17.64 \cdot 2.02 = 35.68N \)


Fg = force in the gravity centre, m = mass of the object, g = acceleration of gravity, α = max acceleration of the system, s = security factor, μ = friction coefficient, k = eccentricity coefficient at force onset, r = distance between prehension and object centre, R = external radius of suction cup, Fr1 = resultant force 1.


In addition to this force, it is necessary to take into account the one due to the circular movement (Centripetal force).

\( F_C = m \cdot w^2 \cdot r \)

\( F_C = 0.462N \)

\( F_s = F_{r1} + F_C = 36.142N \)

\( F_f = F_s / 3+ = 12.05N \)


The chosen value is going to be \( F_f = 13 N \).

The minimum pressure for creating the vacuum would be:

\( P = \frac{F_s}{A_{rectotal}} = \frac{3 \cdot 13}{3 \cdot \pi \cdot 11^2} = 3.42 \cdot 10^{-2} MPa \)


It is needed to calculate the required force to scroll the rails, overcoming the friction force:

\( V_T = V_{H2} + V_{H3} + V_V + V_L \)


We can easily calculate the volume of each part using Inventor’s “IProperties” function.

\( V_T = 1988.322 + 1331.424 + 1603.222 + 390.276 = 5323.244mm^3 \)

\( m_f = 5323.244 mm^3 \cdot 2.7 \times 10^{-6} \frac{kg}{mm^2} = 0.01437 kg \)

\( m = m_f + \frac{1}{3} m_{py} + m_{sc} = 0.01437 + 0.0943 + 0.00323 = 0.1119 kg \)

\( F_r = m \cdot g \cdot \mu_{al} = 0.1119 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 1.7 = 1.864N \approx 2N \)

\( F_t = 3 \cdot F_r = 6N \)

\( V_{H2} \) = volume of link nº2 of the horizontal finger, \( V_{H3} \) = volume of link nº3 of the horizontal finger, \( V_V \) = volume of link nº1 of the vertical finger, \( V_L \) = volume of the latch link, \( m \) = total mass, \( m_f \) = finger’s mass, \( m_{sc} \) = suction cup’s mass, \( m_{py} \) = pyramid’s mass, \( g \) = gravity acceleration, \( \mu_{al} \) = friction coefficient for aluminium, \( F_r \) = friction force, \( F_t \) = total force, \( V_T \) = total volume of the gripper’s fingers.

SOLUTION AND FITTING PARAMETERS FOR ORIENTATION:

The solution proposed is a three-aluminium-claw gripper (one claw per every 120º), each of which is composed by two fingers, that are responsibles of horizontal movement respectively. The vertical fingers are equipped with a suction cup, in charge of secure the pyramid’s movement. For this reason, the first measure to be obtained is the maximum external radius of the suction cup, in order to fit this device perfectly with the pyramid.

We are going to assume the smallest side face area: dmin = 40mm, hmin = 50mm For an optimal grip, the suction cup should grasp the object by its gravity centre, so it is necessary to line up the centre point of the suction cup with the one of the pyramid’s face (the purple point in the figure below).

It is necessary to calculate the maximum radium of the inscribed internal circle, but first we need to obtain the angle “b/2” and then get the value of angle “a”


It is very important to take into account that the maximum suction cup’s radium must be under 14.25mm. Otherwise, the cup could be wider than the pyramid’s face, what would generate a problem when generating the vacuum.


The next point to discuss is the suction cup orientation’s threshold, which depends on the generatrix angle of the pyramid (parameter “α” )


\( \alpha = arctan(arg) = arctan\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{d}{h} \right) \)

\( arg \to 0; \quad \alpha \to 0 \)

\( arg \to \infty; \quad \alpha \to 90 \)


The minimum angle will be reached when \( h_{max} = 60mm \) and \( d_{min} = 40mm \):


\( \alpha_{min} = arctan\left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{40}{80} \right) = 23.41deg \)


The maximum angle will be reached when \( h_{min} = 50mm \) and \( d_{max} = 60mm \):


\( \alpha_{max} = arctan\left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{60}{50} \right) = 46.11deg \)

The next step is to select an appropriate suction cup for performing the job, in this case the selected device is VMECA’s VF20-N-18F suction cup.

This is made by a Nitrile (NBR) flat suction cup with an effective radium of 11mm, fitted by a female G1/8 thread. It has a weight of 3.23 grams, and its dimensions (in millimetres).

Once the geometry is almost completely studied, we proceed to design the gripper hardware.

For correctly performing the auto adjust it is necessary to set a hinge in a piece to link the suction cup with the gripper’s finger (see the hinge’s centre in green, Figure below).


To line up the central point of the suction cup with the last vertical finger’s edge (whose width is 5mm), it is necessary to calculate the length of the latch by the following relation:

\( \sin(43.89) = \frac{5}{r} \Rightarrow r = 7.212 \, mm \)

As the vertical fingers are not mobile, we are going to pick all the pyramids from the same point. In this case the chosen point is the average of the gravity centre’s height:

\( y_{av} = \frac{60 + 37.5}{2} = 48.75 \, mm \)


For obtaining the parameter “v” that is necessary for the vertical finger, we can use the same sketch in the figure below. With the same angle, but now the new parameter “r” is the length of the latch + the length of the suction cup:

\( v = r \cdot \cos(43.89) = (16 + 7.212) = 16.728 \, mm \)


Once “v” is calculated, the length of the vertical finger (“Lv”) is easily obtained by:

\( Lv = 48.75 - 16.728 = 32.02 \, mm \)


The gripper “skeleton” will have the disposition shown in the figure below. As it was said before, the movement is implemented by three rail-based system fingers, that are designed to operate inside the gravity centre’s threshold.

The expected behaviour for the system is the following sequence:

  1. Firstly, the horizontal fingers will expand until they reach the further position possible from the centre of the grip.
  2. The second movement (done by the robot) will set the gripper in the initial position; putting the centre of the gripper in the top vertex of the pyramid.
  3. When this position is reached, the horizontal fingers will contract, and the suction device will slowly spin about the hinge until the system detects that the suction cup’s surface is completely parallel to the pyramid’s face.
