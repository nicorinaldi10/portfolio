What is a Combined and Weighted agorithm for exploration with Turtlebots?

The Combined and Weighted Algorithm is an exploration strategy that integrates multiple methods, assigning different weights to each approach based on real-time efficiency and environmental conditions. This allows Turtlebots to navigate unknown spaces effectively, optimizing coverage and reducing redundant movements.

The algorithm combines frontier-based exploration, where the robot moves toward unexplored areas, with heuristic or reinforcement learning techniques, dynamically adjusting its approach based on sensor data and terrain complexity. This makes it ideal for applications such as search-and-rescue, surveillance, and autonomous mapping.



Algorithm:

The algorithm involves two main steps:

  • Distance Computation: The robot calculates the distance to all valid frontiers and identifies the closest and farthest frontiers relative to its position.

  • Frontier Size Evaluation: The algorithm assesses the size of all frontiers and determines: The largest and smallest frontiers based on their area.

These two factors are weighted and used to determine the most optimal frontier for exploration, ensuring efficient path planning and navigation.
