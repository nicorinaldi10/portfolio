What is IoT and the MQTT Protocol?

The IoT (Internet of Things) refers to the interconnection of devices through the internet to collect and share data in real time. One of the most widely used protocols in IoT is MQTT (Message Queuing Telemetry Transport), a lightweight and efficient messaging protocol designed for communication between devices with limited bandwidth and resources.

In this case, the server is supported by Mosquitto, an MQTT broker that acts as an intermediary in the publish-subscribe model, managing message delivery efficiently and ensuring that data reaches clients subscribed to a specific topic. Due to its lightweight nature and compatibility with multiple platforms, Mosquitto is ideal for testing and deploying in local or cloud environments, enabling reliable communication in applications such as home automation, industry, healthcare, and transportation.


Publisher and Subscriber:

A publisher is a device or application that sends messages to a specific topic, while a subscriber is a device or application that listens to those messages by subscribing to the topic. In this case, two windows will be opened: one acting as the subscriber and the other as the publisher.

This is achieved using the following commands: “.\mosquitto_sub.exe -d -t /[name]” for the subscriber and “.\mosquitto_pub.exe -d -t /[name] -m [message]” for the publisher.
